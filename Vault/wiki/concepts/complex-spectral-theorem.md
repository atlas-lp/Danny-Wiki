# Complex Spectral Theorem

The version of the spectral theorem for complex inner product spaces, which states that an operator is normal if and only if there exists an orthonormal basis of the space consisting of its eigenvectors. A theorem asserting that a normal operator on a complex inner product space has a diagonal matrix with respect to some orthonormal basis of eigenvectors. It is used as a key tool to prove properties of normal and positive operators.
