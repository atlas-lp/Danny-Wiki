# Invariant Subspace

A subspace U of a vector space V is called invariant under an operator T if for every vector u in U, the vector Tu is also in U. A subspace of a vector space V is invariant under an operator T if T maps every vector in the subspace to another vector within that same subspace. A subspace U of a vector space V is invariant under an operator T if for every vector u in U, T(u) is also in U. This concept is used to define restriction and quotient operators and is connected to upper-triangular matrices.
