# AI in Business Speaker Series — Fireside Chat with Sinan Aral and NYU Stern Prof Foster Provost

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

A fireside chat hosted by NYU Stern's Fubon Center (September 2020) featuring Sinan Aral, Director of the MIT Initiative on the Digital Economy and author of *The Hype Machine*, in conversation with NYU Stern Professor Foster Provost. Aral discusses how social media disrupts elections, the economy, and public health, drawing on 20 years of research synthesized in his book. The conversation examines the attention economy, platform business models, the role of engagement as a key metric, and specific evidence regarding Russian interference in the 2016 U.S. presidential election. The discussion bridges a decade of techno-utopianism followed by a decade of techno-dystopianism around social media and AI.

## Key Claims

- Social media platforms operate as an attention economy, monetizing user engagement by selling attention as a precursor to persuasion to brands, organizations, influencers, and even state actors like Russian agents
- Engagement is likely the single most important metric driving platform business models, and the incentives created by these models shape societal outcomes
- Russia's 2016 election interference reached approximately 126 million people on Facebook, 20 million on Instagram, and produced 10 million tweets and 43 hours of YouTube content
- Whether Russian interference tipped the 2016 election depends on three questions: whether the reach and targeting were sufficient, whether it changed voter turnout, and whether it changed vote choice
- *The Hype Machine* represents 20 years of research and four years of writing, and is cited in Netflix's *The Social Dilemma* documentary

Social media attention economy, platform business models and incentives, Russian interference in the 2016 U.S. election, techno-utopianism vs. techno-dystopianism, The Hype Machine book, engagement metrics, AI and the digital economy, election manipulation, social media and public health, persuasion and behavioral change, MIT Initiative on the Digital Economy, The Social Dilemma documentary
