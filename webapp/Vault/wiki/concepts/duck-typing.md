# Duck Typing

A programming concept where the type of an object is less important than whether it has certain methods or behavior, based on the saying 'If it walks like a duck and quacks like a duck, then it's a duck.' A concept in Python's language semantics where an object's suitability is determined by the presence of certain methods and properties, rather than its explicit type.
