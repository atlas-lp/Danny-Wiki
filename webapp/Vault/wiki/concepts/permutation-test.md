# Permutation Test

A resampling procedure for hypothesis testing where results from different groups are combined, shuffled, and then randomly redrawn to determine how often a difference as extreme as the observed one occurs by chance. A resampling procedure where data is randomly shuffled and divided into groups multiple times to determine if an observed difference between samples could occur by chance. A resampling procedure where all data is combined, shuffled, and redrawn into samples many times to calculate a p-value, which can be used for ANOVA.
