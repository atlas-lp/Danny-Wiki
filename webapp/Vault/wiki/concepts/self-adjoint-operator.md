# Self-Adjoint Operator

An operator on an inner product space that is equal to its own adjoint (T = T*), which is analogous to a complex number being a real number. An operator on an inner product space that is equal to its adjoint ($T = T^*$). Every self-adjoint operator is a normal operator. An operator that is equal to its own adjoint. A normal operator on a complex inner product space is self-adjoint if and only if all its eigenvalues are real. A type of operator that is a prerequisite for being a positive invertible operator and is related to the Spectral Theorem.
