## Abstract

Foster Provost delivers a keynote at Strata + Hadoop World Europe 2014 in Barcelona addressing the "dirty secret" of big data: as models built on massive fine-grained data become more powerful, it becomes increasingly difficult to understand why particular decisions are made. He presents techniques for explaining model-driven decisions, including counterfactual evidence approaches, and illustrates them with real-world examples such as corporate residence fraud detection and ad targeting. The talk emphasizes that model interpretability is critical not just for data scientists but for engineers, managers, and other stakeholders who must trust and implement these models.

## Key Claims

- Mining massive fine-grained data can improve predictive performance but creates an interpretability problem — it becomes harder to understand why specific decisions are made
- Multiple stakeholders beyond data scientists (engineers, managers, etc.) need to understand model decisions for models to be effectively deployed
- "Your models are not what your data scientists design, they're what your engineers implement" — communication facilitated by decision understanding is essential
- Counterfactual evidence explanations (showing what evidence, if removed, would change a decision) are a practical technique for explaining model outputs
- Probabilistic models make decisions based on crossing probability thresholds, and adjusting thresholds can yield more detailed counterfactual explanations
- The same explanation approach can reveal potential false positives, as illustrated with a corporate residence fraud detection example involving Rolex
