# Vasant Dhar On Thinking With Machines With Foster Provost

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

This is a fireside chat at NYU Stern (November 17, 2025) where Vasant Dhar discusses his new book "Thinking With Machines: The Brave New World of AI" in conversation with Foster Provost, moderated by Arun Sundararajan. The discussion traces Dhar's personal journey through multiple eras of AI—from early rule-based expert systems through the pivot to machine learning in the early 1990s and its application on Wall Street—while examining AI's progress, reversals, promises, and dangers. The event also includes a demonstration of the Damodaran Bot and addresses what society needs to confront before AI "gets away from us."

## Key Claims

- Early expert systems like Internist required over 10 years of painstaking knowledge engineering by talking to human experts, revealing serious limitations of the knowledge acquisition bottleneck
- The availability of data drove the pivot from rule-based expert systems to machine learning in the early 1990s, enabling machines to learn knowledge directly from data rather than through cumbersome expert interviews
- Explainable AI is not a new concern—rule-based expert systems textbooks from the early 1980s already had multiple chapters on explanation, and many of those capabilities are still not replicated in modern AI
- Dhar applied machine learning to Wall Street, bridging academic AI research with financial applications
- The conversation covers both the promises and dangers of AI, emphasizing issues that need to be addressed proactively
